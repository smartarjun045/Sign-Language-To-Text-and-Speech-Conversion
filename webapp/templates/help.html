How to Use Gesture Voice

Complete guide to sign language recognition and system usage

Getting Started

  1. Click "Start Camera" to activate your webcam and begin the recognition process
  2. Check lighting - Ensure good lighting on your hand for better accuracy
  3. Position your hand clearly in the camera view (centered)
  4. Click "Start Recognition" to begin gesture detection
  5. Make ASL fingerspelling gestures (A-Z) - hold each gesture steady for 1-2 seconds
  6. Watch the text output - recognized characters appear in the text box

Tips for Better Recognition

Good Lighting

Ensure bright, even lighting on your hand. Avoid shadows and backlighting.

Plain Background

Use a plain background (white or light colored) when possible for better hand detection.

Hand Visibility

Keep your entire hand visible in the frame. Ensure no parts are cut off.

Hold Gestures

Hold each gesture steady for 1-2 seconds for the system to recognize it properly.

Consistent Distance

Maintain a consistent distance from the camera (about 30-60 cm away).

Clear Gestures

Make clear, deliberate gestures. Avoid rapid movements between characters.

Special Gestures

SPACE

Creates a space between words in your text

NEXT

Confirms the current character and prepares for the next one

BACKSPACE

Deletes the last recognized character from the text

Troubleshooting

❓ Camera not working?

Check if your webcam is connected and browser has permission to access it. Refresh the page and try again.

❓ Poor recognition accuracy?

Improve lighting on your hand, use a plain background, and ensure your entire hand is visible. Hold gestures steady for 1-2 seconds.

❓ Text not appearing?

Ensure recognition is started and your hand is clearly visible in the camera frame. Try making the gesture more deliberate.

❓ Speech synthesis not working?

Check your system volume and browser settings. The system has a fallback to browser speech synthesis if needed.
